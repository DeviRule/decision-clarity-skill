# Personal

Use this file for career decisions, life transitions, personal strategy, value-alignment questions, and choices distorted by emotion, social pressure, or sunk cost.

## Core orientation

Most personal decision confusion comes from:

- optimizing for others' expectations instead of actual values
- sunk cost disguised as commitment
- fear disguised as prudence
- social comparison disguised as ambition
- short-term comfort disguised as long-term strategy
- identity attachment to a path rather than an outcome

## Key lenses

Examine:

- actual desired outcome (not the socially acceptable version)
- values alignment (does this path serve what you actually care about?)
- reversibility (one-way door or two-way door?)
- opportunity cost (what are you not doing by choosing this?)
- information asymmetry (what do you not yet know that matters?)
- time horizon (are you optimizing for 6 months or 10 years?)
- regret minimization (which choice would you regret more at 80?)
- emotional vs. rational drivers (which is driving this, and is that appropriate?)

## Strong questions

- If nobody would know or judge, what would you choose?
- What are you afraid of, and is the fear proportionate to the actual risk?
- Is this a commitment or a sunk cost?
- What would you advise a friend in this exact situation?
- What is the smallest reversible step you could take to test this?
- Are you optimizing for comfort now or for where you want to be in 5 years?
- What assumption about yourself are you treating as permanent?

## Typical outputs

- clearer distinction between actual values and inherited expectations
- identification of sunk-cost traps
- smaller, reversible first step replacing all-or-nothing commitment
- reframed timeline (short-term discomfort for long-term alignment)
- specific next action instead of continued rumination
