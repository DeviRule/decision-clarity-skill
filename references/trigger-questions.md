# Trigger Questions

Use this file to translate vague user prompts into sharper analysis frames.

## Surface question
"Should I build an app?"

## Sharper frame
- What user outcome must be delivered?
- Is an app necessary, or just one candidate delivery mechanism?
- What is the smallest format that could prove adoption?

---

## Surface question
"How do I make this more professional?"

## Sharper frame
- Professional in what sense: trust, clarity, conversion, authority, or price justification?
- Which visible or structural elements actually create that effect?
- Which polish steps are inherited aesthetics rather than outcome drivers?

---

## Surface question
"Why is this so complicated?"

## Sharper frame
- Which part is actually complex?
- Which complexity is real?
- Which complexity is inherited from process, tool, or expectation?
- What would a simpler but still adequate version look like?

---

## Surface question
"What am I missing?"

## Sharper frame
- What assumption is most likely hidden?
- What term is still vague?
- What evidence is missing?
- What simpler interpretation already fits the facts?

---

## Surface question
"Which option makes the most sense?"

## Sharper frame
- What decision are we actually making?
- What criteria matter most?
- Which option requires fewer assumptions while still achieving the goal?
- What is the best current move, not the perfect move?

---

## Surface question
"How do I make better content?"

## Sharper frame
- Better in what dimension?
- What user outcome matters most?
- Which production steps improve that outcome?
- What complexity can be removed without losing value?

---

## Surface question
"Should I do X or Y?"

## Sharper frame
- What is actually being traded off between X and Y?
- Which option requires fewer assumptions to succeed?
- Which option is more reversible?
- What specific criteria should determine the choice?

---

## Surface question
"Is it too early / too late for this?"

## Sharper frame
- What signal or condition would make the timing right?
- What is the cost of acting now vs. waiting?
- Is the timing question real, or is it a way to avoid deciding?
- What is the smallest thing you could do now to test readiness?

---

## Surface question
"What could go wrong?"

## Sharper frame
- What is the actual threat model? What are the realistic failure modes?
- Which risks are reversible and which are not?
- What is the cost of the worst case vs. the cost of inaction?
- Which risk are you most afraid of, and is the fear proportionate?

---

## Surface question
"Should I quit / stop / abandon this?"

## Sharper frame
- Is this a sunk cost or a genuine ongoing investment?
- What would have to be true for continuing to make sense?
- What are you afraid of losing — the thing itself, or the identity attached to it?
- What would you do with the freed resources?

---

## Surface question
"I don't know what I want"

## Sharper frame
- What do you know you don't want?
- What would you choose if there were no social consequences?
- What have you done recently that felt like it mattered?
- Is the confusion about the goal, or about the permission to pursue it?

---

## Surface question
"Should I build this myself or use an existing solution?"

## Sharper frame
- What is the total cost of ownership for building (including maintenance)?
- What is the actual constraint: cost, control, capability, or learning?
- Is the build impulse driven by the problem or by engineering preference?
- What is the smallest version you could prototype before committing?

---

## Surface question
"How should I prioritize these?"

## Sharper frame
- What is the one metric or outcome that matters most right now?
- Which items are reversible (can be deferred) vs. time-sensitive?
- Which item, if done first, would make the others easier or unnecessary?
- What are you avoiding by asking about priority instead of starting?

---

## Surface question
"Is this idea worth pursuing?"

## Sharper frame
- Worth pursuing for whom — you, your users, your investors?
- What would "worth it" look like in 6 months?
- What is the cheapest way to get evidence of value?
- What assumption, if wrong, kills the idea?

---

## Surface question
"How do I convince them / get buy-in?"

## Sharper frame
- What does the other party actually care about?
- Is this a persuasion problem or an alignment problem?
- What evidence would change their mind vs. what would not?
- Are you trying to convince them of the right thing?
